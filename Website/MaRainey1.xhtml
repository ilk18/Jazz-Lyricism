Meet the Singers

Ma Rainey

Ma Rainey was born April 26th, 1866 to Thomas and Ella Pridgett in Georgia. She loved singing from a young age had showed much promise, especially in her teenage years. She married another performer nicknamed "Pa" Rainey in 1904 and performed with him as a double act. However, their marriage came to an end after almost 12 years and Ma Rainey developed her own show. Ma Rainey, also known as the "mother of blues", rose to fame for her deep voice and amazing stage presence. She was able to write her songs in a way that reflected her sexuality and the way she navigated through life. Being a black female performer was not easy and Ma Rainey often received low wages and small shows. Her segregated audiences flocked to see her perform, especially after her contract with Paramount Records that happened in 1923. In her songs, she sings about sexuality, depression, and other facets of life that she experienced. She inspired many blues singers, especially Bessie Smith.
